Religion Fueled Bias Incidents in the United States in 2019

The U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigations published their 2019 Hate Crime Statistics in which they provided a breakdown of incidents, offenses, victims, and known offenders of hate crimes in the United States in 2019. The breakdown examined statistics for a number of bias motivators including race and ethnicity, religion, sexual orientation, disability, gender, and gender identity. The number of incidents of anti-Jewish bias was astronomically higher than any other religion listed with 953 of the 1,521 total incidents reported. In comparison, the second largest number of incidents resulted from anti-Islamic incidents with 176. Incidents of anti-Jewish bias are significantly higher than any other religious group in the United States for 2019.
